In the Indian subcontinent, the regions of Maharashtra and south Gujarat are covered by the Deccan lava basalt with very low radioactivity; the Gangetic alluvial regions covering parts of Uttar Pradesh, Bihar and West Bengal have somewhat higher radioactivity. In the coastal areas of Kerala and Tamil Nadu, thorium rich monazite sands result in dose rates as high as 32.5 mSv (milli Sievert) per year in some locations.
